FAQ's


How do I create a playlist?

Click Home to start creating a new playlist. First you'll need to enter a name for your playlist and how many songs you want. Then, enter an artist, track and genre you want your playlist to be inspired by - called 'seeds'. You can also add an extra two of the above choices to give you more control over your playlist.

Finally, click Create and your playlist will be generated. All going well, your playlist can be found on the Spotify page of the account you gave permissions with at the start.
If you're still not sure, the Demo gives some sample inputs and the resulting playlist it creates.



How does Blender work?

Blender for Spotify works by harnessing Spotify's recommendation capabilities, and using them to create custom playlists. It is the same capability that Spotify use when they recommend a few songs at the bottom of your playlists, or offer you new tracks in the Discover Weekly playlist.



Why doesn't my playlist match my seeds?

If there are songs in your playlist that don't match your seeds, it is possible that Spotify recognised your input as something different - if you entered 'Animals' as a track seed and wanted Animals by Martin Garrix, Spotify could think you mean Animals by Coldplay. To combat this, you can enter the song title and artist in the Seed Track field to ensure you get the right one, so for Seed Track you could have put 'Animals Martin Garrix'.



Why isn't my playlist showing up?

Firstly, ensure you are connected to the internet on your Spotify app. If you have a lot of playlists, it might be hard to find - try search for your playlist name. When Blender successfully generates your playlist, it will advise you it is done, so check if you can see that message.
